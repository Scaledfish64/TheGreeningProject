The Greening Project

We believe that converting people to clean energy will be the sole determinant of the future for us and our planet, and that it is crucial to execute properly. Our mission is to ensure that you change your carbon footprint to a sustainable level, in a way that best suits your needs.

Our Purpose

The Greening Project’s purpose is to preserve human health and the environment we live in. We educate homeowners about ways that they can reduce their negative impact on the environment, while also benefiting them financially. We provide information about different types of green and clean energy-efficient resources and solutions on our website that can help you decide what you can do to help the environment and profit. We also take your budget into consideration and list possible plans to use green energy effectively. This website also has statistics that show what impact homeowners could have on the environment if they switch to using clean energy solutions. If you are searching for green energy for your home, we can help!

Take Action

Look out for buying appliances with an Energy Star label, they can help you save energy every day. Another product label to look out for is Watersense, which indicates that what you are buying uses less water than is usually expected of that product. Installing solar panels can also help. Participating in a community program, which makes you pay monthly rather than all at once for your solar panels, can help your budget as well. If there is accessible wind energy in your area, you can use that as an alternitive to solar panels. If you want to reduce the impact of your heating and cooling, make sure to check out geothermal heating/cooling devices, which uses much less energy than a traditional AC.

Green Technology

The three main types of clean energy for your house are solar panels, wind energy, and geothermal. Here is some information on all three.
Geothermal: Geothermal heat pumps, as the name suggests, uses the Earth’s natural temperature to balance the temperature inside of homes, in order to heat or cool the area.Geothermal heat pumps can reduce energy consumption and emissions up to 44% compared to air-source heat pumps and 72% compared to standard air-conditioning equipment
Wind: Wind energy can also be one of the most efficient Energy sources if available in your area. It provides electricity without burning any fuel or polluting the air. Wind energy helps avoid 329 million metric tons of carbon dioxide emissions annually.
